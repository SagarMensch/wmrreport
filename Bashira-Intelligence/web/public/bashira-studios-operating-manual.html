Bashira Intelligence Platform Manual

How Decision Studio, Predictive Studio, and Causal Command Work

This document is a client-team operating guide for the three analytic studios in Bashira Intelligence. It explains what each studio is for, which data it uses, what machine-learning or decision logic sits behind it, how each screen should be interpreted, and how the three studios connect into one operating workflow rather than three disconnected dashboards.

1. Platform Operating Model

What the platform is trying to do

The platform is built around three operational questions. Decision Studio answers what is likely to happen across the portfolio. Predictive Studio answers what is happening on one well and where it is heading. Causal Command answers what action should be taken now, on which wells, and how much time could be recovered.

The studios should not be presented as duplicates. They are intended to be used in sequence:

  1. Decision Studio identifies portfolio pressure and model-backed risk.
  2. Predictive Studio validates that picture on a specific well.
  3. Causal Command turns the picture into intervention priority.
Portfolio-level intelligence Well-level operational diagnosis Action and recovery scenarios

Simple mental model

A useful way to explain the platform is:

  • Decision Studio is the portfolio control tower.
  • Predictive Studio is the well investigation room.
  • Causal Command is the intervention desk.
Operational sequence If a client asks “where are we under pressure?”, start in Decision Studio. If they ask “show me this well in detail,” move to Predictive Studio. If they ask “what do we do about it?”, move to Causal Command.
Important discipline Do not present any single screen as the whole product. The strength of the platform is that it links model outputs, operational detail, and intervention choices.

2. Decision Studio

Purpose

Decision Studio is the portfolio-level machine-learning surface. It is designed to answer: which wells are most at risk, what completion timing looks like across the live portfolio, which factors are most strongly associated with progress and completion, and how much confidence the model has in those calls.

This screen is not training models live in the browser. It reads a validated output package generated offline by the Decision Studio training pipeline and placed into the wmr_results artifact folder.

Primary Inputs
WMR + plan + SAP
Historical well progression, plan context, job progress, drilling sequence.
Main Package Files
CSV + JSON + PNG
Metrics, scored wells, feature importance, leaderboard, survival outputs, explainability images.
Model Family
GPU ensemble
CatBoost + XGBoost ensemble with calibration, survival outputs, and explainability package.
Main Question
What is likely?
It is a forecast and ranking studio, not a direct intervention engine.

How the data arrives in the screen

The component loads its outputs from the prebuilt wmr_results package. The core files are:

  • ag_metrics.json for top-line model metrics.
  • training_manifest.json for lineage, thresholds, survival metrics, and model metadata.
  • risk_scores.csv for scored wells and tier assignments.
  • priority_wells_final.csv for the ranked portfolio view.
  • feature_importance.csv for ensemble driver ranking.
  • PNG artifacts for diagnostics, SHAP, rig performance, forecast, and survival visuals.
Key implication Decision Studio is deterministic at display time. If the artifact package is stable, the screen is stable. The heavy ML work happened earlier during the training run.

How the models are constructed

The training pipeline builds multiple outputs, each with a different job:

  • A progress model forecasts near-term movement in well progress.
  • A completion model estimates time remaining and completion windows.
  • A risk model estimates the probability of missing target and maps it into score and tier.
  • A survival block produces completion-probability and hazard-ratio views.

The pipeline also produces calibration, feature importance, SHAP plots, and survival metrics such as the C-index.

What each major Decision Studio panel means

Panel What it is showing How to explain it

Model metrics R², RMSE, MAE, MAPE, AUC, Brier, C-index. “This is how accurately the package performed on validation and survival ranking.”

How the risk score is calculated

At a high level, the score comes from an ensemble miss-target model. The model is trained on historical outcome labels, calibrated, and then mapped into human-usable risk tiers. The live screen presents:

  • Risk probability: calibrated model probability of pressure / miss-target behavior.
  • Risk score: scaled decision score presented in the UI.
  • Risk tier: tier assignment using learned thresholds.
historical features -> ensemble risk model -> calibrated probability -> risk score -> tier thresholds

How to speak about Decision Studio honestly

Decision Studio is strongest when presented as a model-backed portfolio control tower. It is appropriate to say it provides:

  • validated portfolio risk ranking
  • predicted completion windows
  • driver explanation
  • survival-style completion insights

It is not appropriate to say it decides operational actions by itself. That handoff belongs to Causal Command.

3. Predictive Studio

Purpose

Predictive Studio is the well-by-well operational drill-down. It takes the portfolio picture and lets the user investigate a single well in depth. This is where the team sees the well’s history, current phase state, progress forecast, milestones, risk components, and the first causal-style intervention simulator.

Engine
Forecast Engine
CPU-oriented inference engine with historical, latest, and job-progress data.
Main Inputs
History + latest + job progress
Uses historical WMR, live latest snapshot, and job-progress context with live SQL refresh when available.
Primary Question
What is happening on this well?
It is the detailed operational explanation layer.
Outputs
History, forecast, milestones, risk, causal
A single well narrative assembled from live or cached operational data.

What the Forecast Engine does

The Forecast Engine loads:

  • wmr_full_history.csv or live historical SQL for weekly progress history.
  • wmr_latest.csv or live latest SQL for current-state wells.
  • job_progress_gb.csv or live SQL for category plan-vs-actual context.

It normalizes dates and progress fields, builds per-well history, maps well lifecycle phases, and returns a well detail payload containing:

  • current-state summary
  • history points
  • forecast points with confidence bands
  • phase Gantt rows
  • milestone ledger
  • risk breakdown
  • causal intelligence block

How the screen is structured

Predictive Studio has two levels:

  1. A portfolio registry that lists wells with risk tier and summary context.
  2. A well-detail workspace that opens one well and shows its complete operational narrative.

The well-detail workspace is the key part. It contains risk assessment, phase progress cards, predictive trajectory, phase execution, milestone ledger, risk model explanation, and a causal simulator.

Major Predictive Studio sections and what they mean

Section What it shows Operational use
Risk assessment Tier and score for the selected well. Quick view of whether the well deserves attention now.
Phase progress cards Engineering, location prep, construction, OHL, commissioning progress. Shows which phases are advanced and which are lagging.

How the forecast is formed

Predictive Studio’s forecast layer is designed to be CPU-friendly and operational. It uses historical progress series and current state to build a short-horizon progression forecast. The key point is not just the next number; it is the combination of:

  • historical actual line
  • forecast extension
  • confidence interval
  • lineage showing which source fed the chart
Why clients like this screen It converts abstract portfolio modeling into a concrete well story. Teams can inspect a specific well and see how the forecast relates to real milestones and phase execution.

How the risk and causal blocks fit together

Predictive Studio already contains a well-level causal simulator, but this is not meant to replace Causal Command. It acts as a bridge:

  • Decision Studio says the well is risky.
  • Predictive Studio shows the operational state and baseline path.
  • The embedded simulator previews possible action impact on that well.
  • Causal Command generalizes this into a portfolio intervention deck.

4. Causal Command

Purpose

Causal Command is the intervention workspace. It is designed to answer the management question that follows prediction: if these wells are under pressure, which ones should be acted on first, why are they delayed, which governed actions are available, and how much recovery might those actions produce?

Core mode
CPU + Bayesian
Fast decision layer first, deeper Bayesian analysis alongside it.
Live current-state anchor
WellMonitoringReport_Latest
The day-one current-state operating surface for the causal workspace.
Main question
What should we do now?
This is the prioritization and intervention deck, not just another forecast page.
Screen modes
Command / Evidence / Model
Operations view, data traceability view, and model-basis view.

How Causal Command is built

The causal service assembles a real cross-system feature matrix. It joins the live current-state surface with historical and operational support tables, including:

  • WellMonitoringReport_Latest as the current-state anchor.
  • WMR_Full for progression history and stall/regression features.
  • Job_Progress_Report_GB for plan-vs-actual execution pressure.
  • Job_Progress_PlanSnapshot for near-term plan curve concentration.
  • SAP_DRILLING_SEQUENCE for rig movement and drilling field context.
  • task_daily for overdue work and crew/execution pressure.
  • ActivityTaskPlan for remaining planned duration and task progress signals.
  • PH_PRODUCTIVITY_WEEKLY_REPORT and Employee for supervisor-linked productivity context.

Once that unified feature space exists, the service builds a CPU decision layer, scenario rows, support counts, portfolio brief, feature lineage, audit questions, intervention ladder, and a Bayesian deep-analysis block.

What the command view is doing

The command view is the main operating desk. Its structure is:

  • Portfolio brief: top-line management summary cards.
  • Command bar: lets the user search and steer the deck.
  • Priority book: ranked intervention ladder for the highest-value wells.
  • Selected well stage: metrics, lead action, issue, and signal ribbon.
  • 3D pressure surface: portfolio pressure view.
  • 3D scenario frontier: action frontier for the selected well.
  • 3D driver geometry: top signals shaping the call.

This is why the screen should be presented as a decision deck, not a report. It is ranked, interactive, and action-oriented.

What the evidence and model views do

Causal Command also carries two governance views:

  • Evidence view shows join coverage, audit questions, and source-backed proof of what is and is not available.
  • Model view explains engine, row counts, feature counts, RMSE-style basis values, and Bayesian/runtime status.
Why this matters These two views keep the intervention deck defensible. The client can see not just the recommendation, but the evidence surface and model basis behind it.

How the intervention ladder is formed

Each decision object in the ladder is a ranked well-action pair. The service combines current delay, recoverable days, decision score, support strength, signal quality, and scenario availability. The output is a priority book rather than a raw well list. 

live current-state + joined execution signals + scenario support + modeled recovery -> decision score -> ranked intervention ladder

This is why the left rail in Causal Command is operationally stronger than a simple risk list. It is already sorted by actionable recovery value, not just by predicted pressure.

What root causes mean here

Root causes in Causal Command are not generic text labels. They are modeled delay contributors sourced from the causal feature space and linked back to a source table through feature lineage. When the UI shows the top three causes on a selected well, it is presenting the strongest modeled contributors to the intervention call.

This is why the signal ribbon and driver geometry matter. They are the bridge between a ranked action and the underlying operational evidence.

What scenario simulation means

Scenario simulation in Causal Command and Predictive Studio is governed. The system does not allow arbitrary fantasy edits. It works through defined action types and comparable support cases, such as peer execution pace, rig uplift, material readiness improvement, or workfront decongestion.

The output is not “guaranteed causality.” It is a model-backed counterfactual estimate: if the selected action were achieved, what would the new delay path and recovery look like?

5. How the Three Studios Connect

Decision Studio

Job: portfolio forecasting and ranking.

Best question: “Where is the portfolio under pressure?”

Main output: risk tiers, forecast windows, model drivers.

Predictive Studio

Job: well-level operational diagnosis.

Best question: “Show me exactly what is happening on this well.”

Main output: history, forecast, milestones, risk breakdown, local simulator.

Causal Command

Job: portfolio intervention prioritization.

Best question: “What should we act on first, and why?”

Main output: intervention ladder, recovery scenarios, root causes, evidence and model basis.

Recommended operating flow for the client team

  1. Use Decision Studio to identify the wells and clusters carrying the highest current portfolio pressure.
  2. Open the relevant wells in Predictive Studio to validate the operational state, phase progression, and milestone picture.
  3. Move to Causal Command to decide whether the well should be on the intervention deck and which action should be prioritized.
  4. Use Data Integrity in parallel when the client challenges whether the source transaction itself is valid.
Why this sequencing matters It prevents the team from jumping straight from risk visualization to action without first validating operational context.

6. How to Guide the Client Team

How to explain the platform in one minute

“Decision Studio shows the scored portfolio view and where risk is concentrated. Predictive Studio lets us open one well and inspect its real operational history, forecast path, milestones, and risk logic. Causal Command then takes the portfolio from visibility to action by ranking which wells deserve intervention first, why they are delayed, and what recovery may be possible under governed scenarios.”

What not to say

  • Do not say all three studios do the same thing.
  • Do not present Decision Studio as a direct action engine.
  • Do not present Causal Command as proven causal truth in the scientific sense.
  • Do not skip the evidence and model-basis views when trust or data quality is questioned.

Client-facing wording by studio

Studio Safe wording
Decision Studio “This is the portfolio forecasting and risk-ranking layer, backed by the latest validated model package.”
Predictive Studio “This is the well-level investigation workspace, where forecast, phase progress, milestones, and risk are reconciled on one operational narrative.”
Causal Command “This is the intervention deck. It ranks which wells are worth acting on first and estimates recovery under governed scenarios using live operational signals.”
This manual is intentionally detailed so it can be used for internal client enablement, not only for a short demo. If the client team wants even deeper documentation later, the platform already has separate detailed methodology documents for Decision Studio’s ML package and risk-score construction.
