1) End-to-End Runtime Flow

Predictive Studio is not a single model call. It is a multi-stage decision surface. The tab first loads a portfolio-wide well list and risk distribution, then fetches deep detail for a selected well: current state, historical trajectory, Gantt phases, milestones, plan-vs-actual, forecast, ML risk, causal intelligence, and ensemble prediction when available.

2) Predictive Studio UI Architecture

The UI has two operating modes. In portfolio mode it displays a searchable registry of wells plus portfolio KPIs and risk distribution. Once the user clicks a well, the tab switches into a deep-dive workspace with historical progress, future forecast, milestone matrix, Gantt phases, risk drivers, causal root causes, and scenario controls.

4) ForecastEngine: What the Tab Actually Uses

The tab's primary runtime is ForecastEngine. This engine is a live operational synthesis layer. It does not depend on one static model artifact alone. It merges current SQL snapshot state, historical WMR progression, plan-vs-actual records, external ML enrichment, and optional ensemble stacking into one JSON payload.

This design is deliberate: the tab stays operational even when one layer is unavailable, because data access and forecasting are built with fallback behavior instead of a single hard dependency.

5) Time-Series Forecast Logic in ForecastEngine

The tab's native forecast path uses cleaned historical progress and a robust linear trend over recent points. It is intentionally conservative and explainable.

  1. Clean history: duplicate weekly rows are collapsed and cumulative progress is monotonicized with cummax().
  2. Select recent window: up to the last 14 cleaned history points are used.
  3. Convert time to numeric axis: each week is transformed into day_index.
  4. Fit trend: numpy.polyfit estimates slope and intercept for progress vs time.
  5. Forecast 4 weeks: slope is converted to weekly pace and projected forward.
  6. Confidence band: residual standard deviation scales the interval with 1.96 * std * sqrt(i).
  7. Estimated completion: if the slope is positive enough, the engine estimates when progress reaches 100%.

This is not a black-box deep learning forecast. It is a transparent operational trend model suitable for environments where users must understand why the forecast moved.

6) Gantt, Milestones, and Current State Synthesis

Predictive Studio does more than predict one number. It reconstructs a lifecycle model for each well from the latest snapshot.

  • Current state: extracts well identity, rig, cluster, progress, rig dates, engineering and construction dates, commissioning dates, and snapshot timestamp.
  • Gantt phases: maps engineering, location prep, construction, drilling, and commissioning into ordered phase objects.
  • Milestone ledger: converts actual and planned dates into ordered status states: completed, scheduled, overdue, pending.
  • Commissioning placeholder guard: 100% commissioning values without actual commissioning dates are treated as placeholders instead of trusted completion.

7) Risk Logic: Two Different Risk Engines Exist

One of the most important technical distinctions in this product is that Predictive Studio uses two risk layers: a native hybrid risk engine inside ForecastEngine, and a deeper trained-ML risk engine exposed through the CPU ML service and /predict/* APIs.

This layered design is strong engineering. It avoids a single point of failure and lets the product degrade gracefully without losing the predictive surface entirely.

8) Hybrid Risk Composition in ForecastEngine

The tab-level hybrid risk model uses operational evidence directly. Its components include:

  • Schedule variance risk: compares actual progress against where the well should be on the expected curve.
  • Overdue deadline risk: boosts risk when expected rig-off is already missed or too close relative to low progress.
  • Stagnation risk: adds pressure when recent weekly movement is flat.
  • Readiness risk: detects zero-progress or low-preparation states.
  • Execution drag: derived from late rig and phase patterns.
  • Governance risk: absorbs process-control style issues when present.

This produces a hybrid score and tier even before more advanced model services are considered.

9) Exact Kaggle Risk Formula in FeatureEngine

The heavier predictive service keeps a stricter formulaic risk score: Risk = 0.35*progress_risk + 0.25*velocity_risk + 0.20*schedule_risk + 0.20*gap_risk.

  • Progress risk: low completion increases risk.
  • Velocity risk: slower weekly movement increases risk.
  • Schedule risk: days-to-expected-rig-off are normalized into risk.
  • Gap risk: compares expected schedule completion against actual completion.

The output is normalized to 0-100 and mapped into CRITICAL, HIGH_RISK, WATCH, or HEALTHY.

12) Survival Modeling

The Random Survival Forest layer estimates time-to-completion rather than just future progress.

  • Inputs: progress, velocity, remaining work, location prep, construction progress, engineering KPI.
  • Output: median completion week plus early and late intervals.
  • Interpretation: this is useful when the client wants a completion horizon rather than only a progress delta.

13) Causal and Scenario Layer

Predictive Studio includes a causal intervention surface, not only passive monitoring. The deep-dive view receives ml_intelligence.causal_intelligence with root causes, governed intervention actions, baseline assumptions, and methodology text.

14) Ensemble and Diagnostics Layer

The backend exposes an ensemble path that can stack multiple prediction sources: LightGBM-style risk probability from the ML service, StatsForecast-style progress forecast, Stan posterior signals from the causal workspace, and S-Learner CATE outputs when available.

  • Ensemble endpoint: /api/forecast/well/{well_id}/ensemble
  • Diagnostics endpoint: /api/model/diagnostics
  • Purpose: surface model health, active ensemble members, and uncertainty stack status

15) Nightly Evaluation, Anomalies, and Retrain Workflow

The heavier predictive service includes production-style operational ML controls:

  • POST /predict/refresh: nightly batch evaluation across active wells, tier-change detection, anomaly tracker sync.
  • GET /predict/anomalies: returns wells whose risk tier changed relative to prior state.
  • POST /predict/full: queues a full retrain workflow, intended for GPU/cloud execution with Kaggle notebook refresh.
  • GET /predict/model-info: exposes artifact existence, timestamps, and training metrics.

17) Most Important Architectural Insight

Predictive Studio is not one model. It is a layered predictive operating system. The tab-level ForecastEngine provides deterministic, explainable, local well intelligence using live SQL and heuristic forecasting. The adjacent trained-ML service provides richer probabilistic modeling, survival estimates, anomaly tracking, and retrainable artifacts. The UI fuses both into one surface.

That split is a strength, not a weakness. It gives the product both executive reliability and ML depth. If the heavy model path is unavailable, the tab still works. If the heavy path is available, the product gains richer risk, root-cause, and intervention intelligence.

18) Why Forecasting This Domain Is Technically Difficult

Predictive Studio is solving a harder problem than normal business forecasting because oilfield completion delivery is not a clean, stationary time series. Several technical difficulties exist at the same time:

  • Lifecycle heterogeneity: a well passes through engineering, location prep, construction, drilling, and commissioning; each phase has different cadence, blockers, and evidence columns.
  • Sparse and irregular history: weekly WMR snapshots do not always behave like evenly sampled sensor data, so naive forecasting can become unstable.
  • Mixed signal types: progress percentages, overdue dates, categorical rig identity, project priors, and readiness signals all matter together.
  • Censoring and incompleteness: some wells are still active, some stage fields remain blank, and some commissioning dates are placeholders rather than completed facts.
  • Operational dependency chains: a delay is often not local to one field; it may come from rig queue spillover, material readiness drag, or parallel workfront congestion.

This is why the product does not rely on a single straight-line model. It combines direct execution evidence, trend inference, engineered features, survival modeling, and governed causal actions.

19) Why the Product Uses a Hybrid Engine

Layer What It Does Why It Exists
ForecastEngine Builds the live tab payload from SQL or CSV fallback, synthesizes milestones, Gantt phases, plan-vs-actual, and a robust local forecast. Guarantees that the tab can still work in local demo conditions and remains auditable from operational records.
FeatureEngine Runs trained ML inference over 58 engineered features and computes the exact Kaggle-derived risk stack. Adds learned structure, historical priors, and non-linear relationships that pure heuristics would miss.
RSF Survival Model Estimates time-to-completion instead of only next-progress movement. Execs and planners often care more about completion horizon than point progress.
Causal / Scenario Layer Converts risk into intervention choices, counterfactual completion dates, and recovery-day estimates. Turns the tab into a decision system rather than a passive visual report.
Offline artifacts Expose pre-computed portfolio summaries, feature importance, and priority well outputs when live SQL or model infrastructure is constrained. Maintains resilience outside the client VPN and protects the demo path.

The design principle is simple: use deterministic logic where the client needs trust and traceability, and use trained ML where the client needs pattern recognition, probabilistic timing, and hidden-risk exposure.
